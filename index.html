A. P. J. Abdul Kalam

Great Indian Scientist and politician who played a leading role in the development of India's missile and nuclear weapons programs.

About The Legend

A. p. j. abdul Kalam. in full Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam. was born on october 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India

He served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007.

Kalam earned a degree in Aeronaautical Engineering from the Madras Institute Of Technology and in 1958 joined the Defence Research and development Organisation(DRDO).

1969.he moved to the SLV-III, the first satellite launch vehical that was both designed and produced in India.

Rejoining DRDO in 1982, Kalam planned the program that produced a number of successful missiles, which helped earn him the nickname Missile Man

amoung those successes was Agni. India's first intermediate-range ballistic missile, which incorporated aspects of the SLV-III and was launched in 1989.

He also played a pivotal organisational, technical, and political role in India's Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998, the first since the origional nuclear test by India in 1974.

From 1992 to 1997 Kalam was sceintific adviser to the defence minister, and he later served as principal scientific adviser (1992-2001) to the government with the rank of cabinate minister.

His prominent role in the country's 1998 nuclear weapons tests solidified India as a national although the test caused great in the international community.

In 1998 Kalam put forward a countrywide plan called Technology Vision 2020, which he described as a road map for trnsforming India from a less-developed to a developed society in 20 years. The plan called for, amoung other measures, increasing agricultural productivity, emphasizing technology as vehical for economic growth, and widening access to health care and education.

Kalam recieved 7 honorary doctorates from 40 universities. The Government of India honoured him with thePadma Bhushan in 1981 and the Padma Vibhushan in 1990 for his contribution to the scientific research and modernisation of defence technology in India.

While delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong, Kalam collapse and died from an apparent cardiac arreston 27 July 2015, aged 83.

Wheeler Island, a national missile test site in Odisha, was renamed Kalam Island in september 2015.

A prominent road in New Delhi was renamed from Aurangzeb Read to Dr APJ Abdul Kalam Road in August 2015.

In February 2018, scientists from the Botanical Survey of India named a newly found plant species as Drypetes kalamii, in his honour.
